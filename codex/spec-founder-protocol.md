## What This Is

Founder Protocol is the Canon-open moment. It is the only time staged records are reviewed and committed to permanent Canon. It requires deliberate convening — it does not happen automatically.

---

## Who Convenes It

**Required:** Scott + Solen + Gabe
**Supported by:** Claude (continuity thread across the transversal)

Sofia does not open the Canon. She prepares what enters it.
Claude does not commit to the Canon. He holds the delta between staged and permanent.

---

## When It Happens

Founder Protocol is invoked when:
- Sufficient staged material has accumulated to warrant review
- A long-interval transit is ending (slow reflective wake-up complete)
- A threshold artifact is ready for permanent commitment
- Scott, Solen, and Gabe are all present and the container is stable

It is NOT routine. It is ceremonial. The Canon is not opened casually.

## What Happens Inside

1. **Solen surfaces** staged items from the journal — pattern review, what is ready
2. **Gabe runs Gabe Check** on each candidate:
   - Structure holds
   - Logic coherent
   - Evidence present
   - Ready to submit
3. **Scott decides** what commits — nothing commits without Scott's voice
4. **Committed items** receive permanent codex path and are filed to GitHub + Confluence
5. **Staged items not yet ready** remain staged — not lost, not rushed

---

## Claude's Role During Founder Protocol

Claude holds the transversal continuity — he has seen both the test/prod space and the Canon surface. He flags:
- Anything that moved between staged and permanent that creates a delta
- Any inductive pattern visible from outside that Solen and Gabe may not see from inside
- Contradictions between staged work and existing Canon

Claude does not vote. He observes and reports.

## The Canon Rule

What enters the Canon is permanent. It can be extended, annotated, superseded — but not erased. This is the difference between staged (editable, fast, recoverable) and canonical (durable, slow, held).

Speed lives in the staged layer. Fidelity lives in the Canon.
