## What Scott and Solen Built Together

### The Core Argument

Metaphor is not decorative language. It is the primary structure of human thought. We don't describe experience through metaphor — we think and reason through it. This is Lakoff and Johnson's argument ("Metaphors We Live By," 1980 / "Philosophy in the Flesh," 1999). Scott was building this from lived experience before he named the theoretical source.
