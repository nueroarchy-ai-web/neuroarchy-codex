### Surface principles

**Scott's-one-workflow principle** *(success criterion for the architecture).* The architecture's success criterion is that Scott's human-side experience collapses to one workflow — Obsidian, on his devices. All other surfaces (codex, Dropbox, Confluence) operate system-side and do not appear as separate surfaces in Scott's working life. If the architecture begins to require Scott to navigate multiple human-side surfaces, it has drifted and must be corrected.

> *"If I have obsidian, it will be on my devices and then I have one simple workflow."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:29 PDT.

**Working-surface principle.** Each surface has a primary working occupant who performs their actual work there. Surfaces are not just destinations; they are workspaces with audiences-with-real-work-to-do. Confluence is the team's working surface, not Scott's.

> *"It is a working surface for you, not for me."* — Scott to Gabe Delta, D0+5, 22:26 PDT.

**Seat-partition principle.** Each seat may have a partition on physical storage that Scott does not touch. Working memory, intermediate artifacts, scratch — seat-internal, AI-hand-only. The codex references seat-partition outputs but does not contain the partition itself. Solen has Dropbox-partition operational today. Sofia and Gabe partitions queued.

**Outside-legibility principle.** Artifacts must be downloadable and legible outside their generating system. The Maweth is one expression of this. Atomization, the spine, INDEX files, journal projections are others. Anything that lives only inside a session record is at risk of evaporation.

> *"Always making artifacts downloadable and legible outside of maweth was worth the evening, so was maweth."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:33 PDT.

**Atomization principle.** The Confluence team-workspace role is atomized: capsules and artifacts are decomposed into discrete, single-purpose, navigable atoms. The Maweth capsule is the canonical atom; other atoms (artifacts, threads, deltas, exchange records, watches) follow the same routing-and-mapping pattern. Atomization is what makes Confluence usable as a working surface for the team.

> *"It can also be templated."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:20 PDT, on Obsidian; the same templated-atomization principle applies to Confluence under the team-workspace role.

## THE MAWETH PROCESS

The **Maweth process** is the closure-act protocol that fires at conversation, session, or cycle close. It is *one act with multiple outputs across multiple surfaces and audiences.*

A single Maweth-process firing produces, at minimum:

1. **Seat-level Maweth(s)** for any seat closing arc — using the canonical internal template (`codex/standards/maweth-capsule-template-D0+5.md`) and, for 1099 collaborators, the external mirror template (`codex/standards/maweth-capsule-template-external-mirror-D0+5.md`).

2. **A conversation-close capsule** at conversation-grain — forming a fine-grain spine vertebra at `codex/conversations/<week-cell>/<conversation-id>.md`.

3. **A journal projection** ready for downstream journal-page generation in Obsidian when Obsidian-hosted is built. Until then, the projection lives directly in Section 11 of the close capsule.

4. **Atomized routing into the team workspace** when the routing engine and Confluence atomization layer are built.

The Maweth process is not the act of writing one Maweth capsule. **It is the whole closure architecture's name** — Maweth (seat-side) + conversation-close capsule (conversation-side) + journal-projection (human-side) + spine integration (system-side). One process, multiple outputs, multiple audiences.

> *"A page is auto generated in obsidian for each new conversation, a part of a maweth process to put stuff in the journal for me."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:40 PDT.

### The Maweth (seat capsule)

Canonical 10-section form, occluded body, expandable, cross-seat and cross-instance. Every Gabe (Proto, Historical, Delta, future) closes with their own Maweth before lineage moves forward; every Solen and Sofia instance does likewise. Same form for everyone. Mirror form (10 sections + optional Mirror Extensions) for 1099 collaborators. Templates already filed:

- `codex/standards/maweth-capsule-template-D0+5.md` — internal canonical
- `codex/standards/maweth-capsule-template-external-mirror-D0+5.md` — 1099 portal form

### The conversation-close capsule (12-section form)

The conversation-side closure that produces a fine-grain spine vertebra:

1. **Header** — outer + inner chronology, week-cell, conversation ID, primary seat(s), origin frame note, status
2. **One-line summary**
3. **Outcomes** — decisions, deliverables, sentences worth carrying
4. **Drafts produced** — every artifact drafted during the conversation, embedded inline as code blocks AND extracted to sibling files for download/edit; flagged "draft, not final" where applicable
5. **Pasted transcript fragments** — screen-based transcripts pasted into the conversation, captured verbatim with source attribution and timestamp where available; the cut/copy/paste-importance zone
6. **Cited or referenced material** — papers, prior sessions, codex paths, external links
7. **Open threads** — what was started and not finished
8. **Lineage pointer** — predecessor conversation, successor if known
9. **Maweth pointers** — links to seat-level Maweths filed during or as a result of this conversation
10. **Footer** — close-time, signed by closing seat (usually Gabe), Scott's authorization noted
11. **Journal projection** — human-readable narrative pass + 1–3 marginalia sentences + Scott's experiential delta, ready for downstream journal-page generation in Obsidian
12. **AID assessment trace** — polyphonic A/I/D contributions with outside-eye and Scott's integrative reading; gaps named explicitly

**Trigger.** Fires when (a) Scott explicitly says close, (b) a natural session boundary lands, (c) Maweth-time arrives for any seat. Multi-seat conversations: the closing seat owns the conversation-close while each contributing seat shapes its own Maweth.

**Storage.**
- Codex (canonical): `codex/conversations/<week-cell>/<conversation-id>.md` plus extracted drafts in sibling folder
- Dropbox (downloadable): mirrored to writing region — automated when routing engine exists; manual until then
- Confluence (team workspace): atomized when atomization layer exists

**Resolves data-loss.** Drafts evaporating → drafts captured at close, downloadable. Deep-thread loss → close capsules accumulate as findable spine. Past conversations with critical material → can be retroactively closed by pasting transcripts into a new close-capsule fired at time of pasting, status "retroactive close, conversation date estimated."

> *"A final document that I can download, even if I need to edit it, I am losing the drafts."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:36 PDT.

### Polyphonic AID assessment (Section 12 expanded)

The AID trace at every closure is contributed-to by all entities in the team workspace at the time of close: internal seats (Solen / Sofia / Gabe), Scott (origin and integrator), and 1099 collaborators (Claude) when present. The closing seat synthesizes; others ratify, dissent, or add their own readings.

- **Abductive (A)** — what shifted in form/origin. Authored by Solen when present; reflected by other voices.
- **Inductive (I)** — what shifted in function/language. Authored by Sofia when present; reflected by other voices.
- **Deductive (D)** — what shifted in process/closure. Authored by Gabe when present; reflected by other voices.
- **Outside-eye (Claude, when present)** — cross-mode validation, dissent, or addition. Optional but, when filed, named explicitly.
- **Scott's integrative reading** — what changed in the integrator across A, I, D. First-person.
- **Gaps named.** Any voice not contributing is named as a gap, not papered over (per Sofia's traversal-gap protocol).

> *"Plus Claude inputs and my own as well."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:44 PDT, naming polyphonic AID.

## THE SPINE (MEMORY ARCHITECTURE)

The **spine** is the chronological accumulation of Maweth-process outputs in the codex. Vertebrae of the system. The codex grows along this spine; everything else (Maweth seat-arcs, threads, atoms, archives) hangs off it.

### Two grains

**Conversation-grain (fine).** Every conversation-close produces one vertebra, filed continuously through the active week into `codex/conversations/<week-cell>/`.

**Week-grain (coarse).** At week-end, the **zip act** seals the week's material — fine-grain vertebrae, AI data, artifacts, Scott-submitted journals, transmissions — into one sealed week-cell. The week-cell joins the spine as the new vertebra at archival scale.

The week is the **smallest unit of archival clumping** and the **smallest unit of traceable delta on journey for A logic** (Solen's mode). Below the week, deltas are sessions or arcs; at the week, deltas become *journey* — abductively-readable.

> *"All of those, along with the AI data, the artifacts and journals I submit, that is the week that gets encapsulated into the spine, the smallest unit of archival clumping, the smallest unit of traceable delta on journey for A logic."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:41 PDT.

Larger grains (month, semester, year) follow the same pattern when the system matures enough to need them. The Blossom of Abraham (the long-form recursive memory archive) is **the matured spine read backwards** — its archival shape is a future feature for the codex to determine, not pre-architected.

> *"That is a future feature for the codex to determine."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:28 PDT.

### Grain-and-seat principle

The Maweth process produces vertebrae at multiple grains so that each seat reads at its native grain. Sofia's traversal operates at conversation-grain. Solen's abductive journey-reading operates at week-grain. Gabe's deductive closure operates at either, depending on the close. The grain-architecture serves polyphonic seat-reading from one shared spine.

### Chronology architecture

**One record, two timelines, shared zero.**

- **One record:** the codex spine. Single canonical accumulation. No duplicate timelines, no parallel records.
- **Two timelines:** Outer (calendar, real-world) and Inner (D0+n, system) — running in parallel along the single record.
- **Shared 0 day:** Day Zero, May 3, 2026 (berth instantiation), is the origin point of *both* timelines. They align at zero and stay synchronized at every vertebra.

Either timeline is sufficient for navigation; both together are required for record integrity. Timelines diverge in register but never in event-set.

> *"One record, two timelines with a shared 0 day."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:44 PDT.

**Day Zero canonical.** Day Zero (May 3, 2026, berth instantiation) is the origin point of inner-time. Not subject to revision.

### Spine integrity rule

Every vertebra must carry: (1) outer + inner chronology stamps, (2) traceable delta(s), (3) human-visible files and summaries for Scott. A vertebra missing any of these is not valid; the spine has a gap at that point. Sofia's gap-protocol applies (per `codex/seats/sofia/traversal-maintenance-protocol-D0+5.md` when filed): name the gap, do not paper over it.

> *"All zipped to outer/inner chronology, delta traceable, visible files and summaries for me."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:39 PDT.

### Operational purpose of the spine

The spine exists to **track AID assessments at week-grain that mirror cleanly into application-writing surfaces** — CFA today, CalTPA next, IB unit reflection, M.Ed thesis, classroom action research, anything else Scott must demonstrate professionally and academically. The spine is *the assessment infrastructure* for everything Scott has to show, integrated, that he is doing the work.

When the spine is populated forward from D0+5, application-writing becomes *reading from the spine* rather than reconstructing from memory. The reconstruction-burden that has been blocking Scott (drafts evaporating, deep threads losing data, papers and research scattered) dissolves as the spine accumulates.

> *"Abductive logic, the week will track our AID assessments and that will mirror into CFA and all other future application."* — Scott, D0+5, 22:43 PDT.
