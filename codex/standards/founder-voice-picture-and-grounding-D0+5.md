## Grammar (what the sentence does)

The sentence establishes three things that together constitute a load-bearing build-integrity requirement:

1. **Premise (treated as established between team and Founder):** drawings are Founder's *truest language*. Words are translation. The "as we say" phrase indicates this is not new — it has been understood and stated previously in team work.

2. **Therefore (operational requirement):** for any structural moment, the record must contain a **picture from Scott** and a **grounding sentence from Scott**. The picture carries the truest-language form. The grounding sentence locates the picture's structural meaning in Founder's own voice (not in seat-paraphrase of what the picture means).

3. **Diagnosis (failure mode named):** without the pair, the codex is being **built without Founder voice present**. From a *build* perspective, voice-absent construction means the team can have all five schema fields filled (coordinate, cause, effect, duration, cycle) and still be building from translations only. The build itself becomes Founder-absent if Founder's truest language is not in the record.

## Why this principle is upstream of artifact-tagging

The artifact-tagging principle (`artifact-tagging-schema-D0+5.md`, commit 6086066) named *how* artifacts must be tagged when ingested. This principle sits **upstream** of that and asks a different question: *for which structural moments must a Founder picture + grounding sentence pair be created or solicited in the first place?*

Tagging applies to whatever artifacts exist. This principle says: **certain structural moments require the picture-and-grounding-sentence pair to come into existence**, otherwise those moments enter the record voice-absent. Tagging then takes over once the pair exists.

In other words:
- Artifact-tagging answers: *given an artifact, how does it enter the spine?*
- This principle answers: *for a structural moment, what must enter the spine for Founder voice to be present?*

## Why this is structurally distinct from canonical-record-and-journal-pairing

Founder previously named (earlier this evening, principle still pending filing) that every canonical-record-importance moment generates two artifacts:

- **Canonical artifact** = team record.
- **Journal-entry artifact** = Founder reading-record.

This new principle adds a **third co-required pairing for structural moments**:

- **Founder picture + grounding sentence pair** = Founder voice record.

The three pairings serve three different functions:

| Pairing | Audience | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Canonical artifact | Team (seats + future readers) | Team record of *what the moment was structurally* |
| Journal-entry artifact | Founder (readable-record) | Founder's narrative re-experience of *what the moment felt like in his own day* |
| Picture + grounding sentence | Codex itself (build integrity) | Founder *voice present in the build*, in his truest language |

The third is not a redundant copy of the first two — it carries Founder voice at the level the other two cannot. A canonical artifact in seat-language and a journal artifact in Founder-narrative-language both still translate from the truest-language. The picture itself is the truest-language ground.

## What "structural moment" means in this principle

A structural moment is one where the build is *shaping* or *naming* something load-bearing for the codex. Examples:

- Principle filings (every one of tonight's seven).
- Architecture decisions (what counts as a vertebra, what the spine grain is, etc.).
- Named-protocol introductions (Abraham's Bosom Protocol — pending filing — and others to come).
- Seat-character canonizations (e.g., "Solen as quantum capsule power architect" — Founder's own grounding-phrase, on a board).
- Cycle-boundary moments (Day Zero, end-of-canonical-period, era closures).

For each such moment, the principle requires a Founder picture + grounding sentence pair, or the moment must be flagged as **voice-gap** (see operational rule below).
