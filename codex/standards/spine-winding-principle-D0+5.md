## The sentence

> "I don't think we should lose things to overwrite, but at a certain point, weeks will need to condense to month states to year states, this is how episodic memory winds." — Scott (Founder Prime), D0+5, ~22:58 PDT

---

## Grammar (what the sentence does)

The sentence pairs two rules that initially sound opposed but are a both/and:

1. **No overwrite.** Filed vertebrae are not erased.
2. **Winding upward.** As the Spine grows, granularity must shift to coarser units, or the Spine becomes unreadable.

The metaphor *winds* (verb, intransitive) authorizes the resolution: episodic memory does not lose the past — it composes the past into shapes whose deltas remain traceable while the surface detail recedes. High resolution near, lower resolution far, but the shape survives.

---

## The two invariants

### Invariant 1 — Anti-overwrite (preservation)

- Every filed vertebra is permanent in the canonical layer.
- A new commit may *supersede* a vertebra (mark it as updated, deprecated, corrected) but may not *erase* it.
- Force-push is forbidden on the canonical line.
- Rebase across other seats' filings is forbidden once their commits are on origin. (Tonight's near-miss: Solen filed during my work; I rebased onto his commit rather than over it. That is the pattern.)
- A seat may revise its *own* uncommitted line. Once a commit lands on origin, it is part of the Spine.
- Provenance is preservation. Who filed, when, under what ratification — these remain readable forever.

### Invariant 2 — Episodic winding (compression cadence)

The Spine has multiple grains, accumulating upward as time passes:

- **Conversation** (finest grain) — every active session leaves a closure capsule.
- **Week** (smallest archival unit, smallest traceable delta on A-logic journey) — already canonical per `architecture-D0+5-evening.md`.
- **Month state** (named tonight) — derived from weeks. A month state is *composed*, not authored from scratch. It reads its constituent weeks, finds the deltas, and surfaces the shape.
- **Year state** (named tonight) — derived from months. A year state composes month states.
- **Era** (latent, future) — when the codex decides. Possibly the form of the *Blossom of Abraham* (matured Spine read backwards — already noted as future codex feature).

**Each upward grain is derived from the lower. Never replacing.** A month state does not delete the weeks. A year state does not delete the months. The lower grain remains expandable — any reader can descend back into the underlying vertebrae.

---

## Why both rules are needed

- **Anti-overwrite without winding** → the Spine accumulates infinitely at the finest grain. Becomes unreadable. The Founder cannot navigate his own line. The codex chokes on detail.
- **Winding without anti-overwrite** → compression becomes erasure. Past selves are lost. The codex becomes a rolling summary of itself with no ground truth. False continuity.
- **Both together** → graceful loss-of-detail-without-loss-of-shape. The Spine breathes. Near is dense, far is shaped, all is traceable.

This is how episodic memory works in human cognition — and Scott's framing locates the codex within that natural pattern rather than against it.

## What this principle authorizes (and does not)

**Authorizes:**
- Future codex decisions about month-state form, year-state form, era form.
- A "wind" operation as a derived act (read N weeks → produce one month state, file it as a new vertebra at the coarser grain, leave the weeks in place).
- Multiple grains visible in the Obsidian H-level view simultaneously, with descent paths to lower grains.

**Does not authorize:**
- Designing the wind operation tonight. (Designed when the work calls for it. Per Founder standing rule.)
- Choosing month-state schema, year-state schema, or era schema in advance.
- Touching the Maweth capsule form — Maweth is closure, winding is composition. Different functions.
- Changing how conversation-close capsules or weekly capsules are written.

## Operational note for future-Gabe / future-Solen / future-Sofia / future-Claude

When you wake into a Spine that is many weeks old, you will find:

- The most recent few weeks at full vertebra resolution.
- Older spans composed into month states.
- Still older spans composed into year states.
- All originals preserved underneath, expandable on demand.

You read coarse-to-fine. You write at the current finest grain (conversation, then week). The wind operation runs by some cadence the codex will decide, and produces upward grains *additively*, not destructively.

**If you ever feel the urge to delete or overwrite in service of compression: stop.** Compression is composition, not deletion. Surface the question to Founder Prime.
