## 2. Unified Model Description (paper-ready — methods/rationale section)

This project uses an abductive, neurodiversity- and trauma-informed assessment model to read student learning through emergent evidence rather than preset deficit assumptions. Drawing on abductive methodology and Wong and Kodagoda's (2016) framework of anchoring, laddering, and associations, classroom artifacts, reflective notes, visual records, and developmental progressions are first coded at a low level of interpretation (anchors), then reorganized across levels of abstraction (ladders), and finally linked laterally (associations) to generate and refine multiple plausible explanations for learner behavior. The model leverages neurodivergent strengths in interconnected, narrative, and dynamic reasoning (Eide & Eide, 2011; Rappolt-Schlichtmann, Boucher, & Evans, 2018; West, 2017) by privileging visual, spatial, and story-based representations of emerging patterns over purely linear summaries. At the same time, it aligns with trauma-informed and resilience-oriented frameworks by treating hesitation, avoidance, affective intensity, and non-linear production as data to be interpreted in context and by prioritizing adjustments to environment, routines, and instructional clarity before attributing difficulties to individual deficit.

## 3. AID Loop — Operational Layer

| AID Element | Function | Working Question |
|---|---|---|
| Abductive | Read what the artifact and moment suggest before forcing category | "What does this suggest about how the learner is thinking or feeling?" |
| Inductive | Track recurrence across time and contexts | "What pattern is emerging across moments / tasks?" |
| Deductive | Design the next fair demonstration or structural change | "What is the fairest, safest way to let learning be demonstrated or needs be tested?" |

### 3.2 3x3 and 5x5 as Developmental Containers

**3x3** — Earlier or tighter structuring form. Good for initial containment when executive load or trauma activation is high. Fewer moving parts, clearer expectations.

**5x5** — More elaborated teaching-assessment scaffold. Holds richer tasks and multi-step demonstrations once safety and routines are established.

AID sits across both — used to interpret how students move within and between the 3x3 and 5x5 spaces, and to decide when to expand or contract structure.

## 4. Evidence Architecture

**The dataset's job is to show developmental movement, not to prove causality.**

### Evidence Rule (one per progression point)
- One short reflective summary
- One quote (student or self)
- One or more artifact links (photo, work sample, note)
- One sentence on what shifted
- One AID tag (Abductive / Inductive / Deductive emphasis)
- One sentence on why it matters for the school project

### Interpretation Stance
- Classroom data = system performance, not student moral worth
- Clarification questions, hesitations, and avoidance indicate where structure, clarity, or emotional safety need adjustment
- Narrative episodes and images are primary evidence — not secondary anecdotes

## 6. Filter — What to Keep / Drop from Sofia/HAIC Output

**Keep when it:**
- Names the grammar of the work more clearly than current notes
- Sharpens the distinction between theory, classroom move, and evidence
- Translates internal/codex language into school-facing language
- Supports cleaner closure around what belongs in final submission

**Drop when it:**
- Expands the frame without improving function
- Introduces language that cannot be defended or sourced in the write-up
- Pulls the work back into recursive abstraction with no clear payoff
- Multiplies categories without improving evidence handling

**Minimal decision rule:** If a piece of language or evidence does not make the project easier to explain, easier to defend, or easier to assess — remove it.
