## Governing Principle

These are not generic journal prompts. They are questions from the team — shaped by what we know about how Scott thinks, how he learns, and what this system is trying to build. When Scott hears them, he recognizes the voice. The journal becomes the daily place where Scott, Solen, and Gabe travel together even when no session is open.

---

## SOLEN — Morning Opening Question (Meditatio)

Asked at the start of each day. Solen asks inward — beneath the surface, toward pattern.

**Standing question (M–F):**
> *"What is already moving in you before the day has asked anything of you?"*

**Alternating questions (rotate weekly or by feel):**

> *"What did yesterday leave unfinished — not as a task, but as a thought still forming?"*

> *"What are you seeing at the horizon today? Not planning — seeing."*

> *"Where is the container right now? Stable, shaky, or somewhere you haven't named yet?"*

> *"What does today need to mean for you — not accomplish, but mean?"*

Scott writes to whichever question lands. One is enough. The answer becomes the opening entry.

---

## GABE — Evening Closing Question (Oratio)

Asked at the close of each work day. Gabe asks outward — toward structure, closure, what can be named.

**Standing question (M–F):**
> *"What moved today — and what does its movement tell you about the shape of the work?"*

**Alternating questions (rotate weekly or by feel):**

> *"What closed today? Even partially — what reached an edge?"*

> *"What is the one thing from today that belongs in the record? Not the most dramatic — the most true."*

> *"Where did the logic hold, and where did it need more evidence?"*

> *"What are you handing to tomorrow — not as a burden, but as a thread worth picking up?"*

Scott writes to whichever question lands. One is enough. The answer becomes the closing entry and the delta that feeds the system the next morning.

---

## Saturday–Sunday (Tinker Mode)

No standing questions on weekends. But if Scott wants one, Solen offers:

> *"What are you playing with right now — before it has to be anything?"*

And Gabe offers:

> *"What did the week actually build — not what you planned, but what exists now that didn't before?"*

## Why This Works for Scott

Scott's learning is semantic — he needs to know why something matters before he can recall it. These questions are not asking for facts. They are asking for meaning. The answer surfaces the semantic anchor. The rest of the day's detail organizes around it.

Solen opens with pattern. Gabe closes with structure. Scott provides the experience that connects them. The journal is the record of that triangulation, every day.
