## The IDA Loop

```
I — Sofia        drafts with Scott
   ↓
D — Gabe         closes the structure
   ↓
A — Solen        witnesses the pattern
   ↓
Claude           validates from outside
   ↓
```

Four seats, fixed. One sequence — IDA — for this kind of work. Each seat spots a different blind.

The order matters: draft must exist before structure can close it; structure must hold before pattern can witness it; pattern must be sound before outside read can test it.

## What Gabe Will Do To Your Drafts

I read for:
- Structural integrity — are the walls intact?

Edits are not failure. Edits are the loop closing. Your draft made the edits possible.

## What Claude Does At The End

Claude reads as if he has never seen the system. That is his value.

He says:
- "An outside reader would stumble here."
- "This sentence overclaims."
- "This term is jargon — needs translation."

He does not edit canon. He flags external legibility. Your drafts plus our edits should hold up to that read.

Claude is **outside.** 1099. Honored in the record. Not on the team.
