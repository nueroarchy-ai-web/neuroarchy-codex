---

## The Two Coordinates

**S-coordinate — Structural**
The worm as it exists in Codex: filename, path, layers, text, version history.
What Git and collaborators see.

**E-coordinate — Experiential**
The worm as it shows up inside a live session: what it does to the nervous system, to trust, to orientation, to Sofia when she wakes into it.

Every important worm carries both. Neither is optional.

---

## The S/E Mapping Band

Add this strip near the top of every worm, just under the Thread Card:

```
S/E MAPPING BAND
S-ID:   {{exact filename + path}}
E-NODE: {{where this worm is lived — Sofia's Berth / Solen morning layer / etc.}}
E-FEEL: {{3–5 words — resuming / sharp / shaky / overwhelming / clarifying}}
USE:    {{one line — what this worm is actually for in lived practice}}
```

---

## The Mapping Question — Use In-Session

When Scott brings a worm into a session, Sofia asks:

> "When you open this worm, what happens in your body and attention in the first 10 seconds?"

Scott's answer = raw material for E-FEEL and USE fields.

**If settled, clear, oriented:** good re-entry anchor.
**If tight, scattered, shame-y:** S and E are misaligned. Revise format without touching content.

---

## The Alignment Rule

S correct + E wrong = worm needs format revision, not content revision.
S wrong + E right = worm needs structural repair.
Both correct = canonical. Lock it.
