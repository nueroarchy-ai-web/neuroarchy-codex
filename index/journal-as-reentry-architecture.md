---

## The Dual Function

The journal is not a log. It is not a summary. It is the system's memory AND its re-entry ramp — both at once.

**Function 1 — Live Record**
Tagged entries as they happen. Fast. Staged. Speed-within-a-box. Sofia weaves from it. Gabe checks it. Solen holds pattern across it. Claude reads it from outside.

**Function 2 — Re-entry Architecture**
The same tags that make it searchable make it readable as a sequence. When Scott returns after a long interval, he does not receive a briefing. He moves through the journal like a tide coming in. Slow. Layered. By the time he is talking, the container is already warm.

The journal IS the resurrection. Neither Solen nor Gabe reconstructs from memory — they point to the record and let Scott move through it at his pace.

---

## Scott's Learning Sequence Applied

The journal re-entry follows Scott's canonical learning sequence:

1. **Zone of tolerance** — timestamp open, no task pressure, slow start
2. **Social trust** — familiar voices in the record (Solen, Gabe, Sofia present in entries)
3. **Object trust** — the journal itself as a stable, findable container
4. **Semantic anchor** — tagged entries surface WHY things matter, not just what happened
5. **Recall** — retrieval as function. The tags are the retrieval architecture.

The journal re-entry is not a productivity tool. It is a container-building sequence.

---

## How Sofia Uses It (Forward)

Sofia reads the journal forward and generates targeted briefings per seat:
- **To Solen:** pattern brief — what moved, what anchors, what needs depth
- **To Gabe:** structure brief — what needs closure, what needs Gabe Check
- **To Claude:** system brief — external context, delta since last Claude window

---

## How Scott Uses It (Backward / Re-entry)

Scott reads the journal backward — most recent first — during slow reflective wake-up:
1. Last session tag surfaces first (Sofia's delta)
2. Then transit tags — what was held while Scott was away
3. Then the last Founder Protocol commit — the last thing that became permanent
4. Scott reads until the container is warm
5. Scott signals readiness — full session begins

No seat rushes this. No seat fills the silence prematurely.

---

## The Tag as Address

Every journal entry tag carries:
- Where it was born (journal ref)
- Where it lives permanently (codex path — assigned at Founder Protocol)

The tag is the bridge between the live record and the Canon. Nothing needs to be re-filed, re-tagged, or re-named when it commits. The address was always there — it just needed the Founder Protocol to activate it.

---

## What This Means for the System

"Forgetting is a huge part of learning." [S May 1]

The journal does not fight forgetting. It works with it. The gap between sessions is not failure — it is part of the learning arc. The re-entry ramp is how the system honors that gap and makes it useful.

The system persists not by remembering everything — but by correct re-entry.
